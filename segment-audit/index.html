About the Platform

Field Data That Drives Infrastructure Change

CycleAudit is Parisar's internal auditing tool used by surveyors on the ground to document the real condition of Pune's cycle tracks. Every obstruction, broken surface, missing ramp, and dangerous intersection is recorded, scored, and converted into advocacy evidence.

🗺️

Geo-Referenced Segments

Roads are divided into precise 500m segments with GPS coordinates, ensuring complete and consistent coverage.

📊

Multi-Dimensional Scoring

Safety, Continuity, and Comfort — three independent dimensions measured from real field observations and combined into a final weighted score.

📄

Professional PDF Reports

Auto-generate comprehensive stakeholder reports with segment scores, observations, and actionable recommendations.

🔒

Surveyor Accounts

Each audit is linked to a registered surveyor, enabling accountability and historical tracking of audit data over time.

🚴
Why Cycle Track Audits Matter
Pune has invested crores in cycle track infrastructure, yet most tracks remain underused or inaccessible. Systematic audits create a factual baseline that Parisar uses to advocate with the PMC for targeted repairs, encroachment removal, better lighting, and safer intersections.

The 2024–25 audit found poor continuity, dangerous intersections, and encroachment as the three most common issues — all documentable through this platform.

2024–25 Audit Findings

What the Field Data Revealed

Parisar's 2024–25 Cycle Track Audit uncovered critical deficiencies across Pune's cycle network — findings that drive the case for urgent infrastructure intervention.

⚠️
11%

Rated "Good"

Only 11% of 77 audited segments met the threshold for Good quality — safe, continuous, and comfortable for everyday cyclists.

🚧
60%+

Have Obstructions

More than 60% of segments recorded fixed or movable obstructions blocking the cycle track, from hawkers to utility panels.

🔀
40%

Missing Ramps

Nearly 40% of intersection points lack proper on/off ramps, forcing cyclists to dismount — a critical continuity failure.

💡
35%

No After-Sunset Lighting

A third of all segments have no functional lighting after sunset, making night cycling unsafe and discouraging commuters.

🛣️
22%

Missing Track Sections

22% of segments have portions where the cycle track is entirely absent — forcing cyclists onto vehicular traffic lanes.

🚶
55%

Pedestrian Conflict

More than half of all segments reported pedestrians regularly walking on the cycle track, reducing usable width.

What Gets Audited Per Segment

Start/end landmarks · GPS coordinates · Fixed obstructions (12 types) · Movable obstructions (6 types) · Parked vehicle causes (6 types) · Track surface issues · Overhead obstructions · Intersection data · Footpath rating · Lighting · Shade · Buffer zone · Geometry type · Dimensions · Surveyor comments

🔒

Segment Locking

Once a segment is submitted, it is automatically locked to prevent duplicate entries. Surveyors can view submitted data but cannot overwrite it — ensuring data integrity across the entire audit.

🎯

Road-Level Final Score

Once all segments are audited, a weighted final score (0–100) is calculated using segment lengths as weights. Roads scoring below 50 are rated "Poor" and flagged for priority intervention.

Scoring Methodology

How Scores Are Calculated

Each segment receives three independent scores — Safety, Continuity, and Comfort — which are averaged into a final score from 0 to 100.

Rating Scale
80–100 → Good — Safe and usable for everyday cycling
50–79 → Moderate — Usable with significant issues
0–49 → Poor — Unsafe or unusable for most cyclists
Final Score = 100 minus Average Penalty across Safety, Continuity, and Comfort
